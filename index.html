Wine Regions of the World

Explore the regions where Ardoa's wines are produced. Each region has unique terroir, climate, and winemaking traditions.

🇫🇷 France

Beaujolais

Burgundy Region, Eastern France
Beaujolais lies just south of Burgundy, producing wines exclusively from Gamay grapes. Beaujolais Villages comes from 38 designated villages with superior terroir. The region pioneered carbonic maceration, creating wines that are fresh, aromatic, and lower in tannin - perfect for enjoying slightly chilled.
Climate Continental
Key Grape Gamay
Our Wine from Here
Beaujolais Villages Marine Decome 2023

Côtes du Rhône

Rhône Valley, Southern France
The Southern Rhône is known for warm Mediterranean climate and GSM blends (Grenache, Syrah, Mourvèdre). The region's signature "garrigue" - wild herbs like thyme, lavender, and rosemary - grows among the vines and infuses the wines with their aromatic character. Plan de Dieu is a named village within Côtes du Rhône Villages, known for powerful wines from the "Plain of God."
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grapes Grenache, Syrah
Our Wines from Here
Château de Trignon Côtes du Rhône 2022, Domaine Verquière Plan de Dieu 2024

Loire Valley

Central France
France's longest river creates diverse wine regions. Bourgueil produces elegant Cabernet Franc reds on tuffeau limestone. Pouilly-Fumé makes world-class Sauvignon Blanc with flinty minerality. Anjou specializes in versatile Chenin Blanc. The cool climate preserves acidity and creates wines with fresh, vibrant character.
Climate Cool Continental
Key Grapes Cab Franc, Sauv Blanc, Chenin
Our Wines from Here
Domaine Les Pins Bourgueil, Pouilly-Fumé Dezat, La Petite Roche Anjou

Bordeaux

Southwest France, Atlantic Coast
The world's most famous wine region, Bordeaux is divided by the Gironde estuary into Left Bank (Cabernet Sauvignon dominant) and Right Bank (Merlot dominant). Maritime climate moderates temperatures. Bordeaux Supérieur has stricter requirements than basic Bordeaux - lower yields, higher alcohol, longer aging.
Climate Maritime
Key Grapes Merlot, Cab Sauv
Our Wines from Here
Château Roc Meynard Bordeaux Supérieur

Languedoc (Pays d'Oc)

Southern France, Mediterranean
France's largest wine region by area, the Languedoc is a hotbed of innovation. Warm Mediterranean climate and diverse soils create bold, characterful wines. The region pioneered orange wines in France - white grapes fermented with skin contact like red wine, creating amber color and tannic texture.
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grapes Grenache, Sauv Blanc
Our Wine from Here
Les Fumées Blanches Orange 2024

🇮🇹 Italy

Piedmont (Monferrato)

Northwest Italy
"At the foot of the mountains," Piedmont is Italy's most prestigious region for red wines. While famous for Nebbiolo (Barolo, Barbaresco), Barbera is the workhorse grape—high acidity, low tannins, bright cherry fruit. Monferrato's rolling hills and continental climate produce food-friendly wines perfect for the region's rich cuisine.
Climate Continental
Key Grapes Barbera, Nebbiolo
Our Wine from Here
Ercole Barbera del Monferrato 2024

Chianti Classico, Tuscany

Central Italy
The historic heart of Chianti, distinguished by the Black Rooster (Gallo Nero) seal. Chianti Classico requires minimum 80% Sangiovese, creating wines with bright cherry fruit, high acidity, and savory character. The zone between Florence and Siena produces more structured, age-worthy wines than regular Chianti.
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grape Sangiovese
Our Wine from Here
Rocca di Castagnoli Chianti Classico 2022

Abruzzo

Central Italy, Adriatic Coast
Abruzzo lies on Italy's Adriatic coast, sheltered by the Apennine mountains. The Montepulciano grape (not to be confused with the Tuscan town) thrives here, producing deeply colored, soft wines with dark fruit and herb notes. One of Italy's best value regions, Abruzzo offers quality wines at accessible prices.
Climate Mediterranean/Mountain
Key Grape Montepulciano
Our Wine from Here
Colle Corviano Montepulciano d'Abruzzo 2023

Delle Venezie (Veneto/Friuli)

Northeast Italy
This large IGT covers Veneto, Friuli, and Trentino in Italy's northeast. Cool Alpine influences create crisp, refreshing whites. Italian Pinot Grigio here is made in a light, neutral style—fermented in steel to preserve freshness. The region's proximity to Austria and Slovenia shows in its emphasis on aromatic white varieties.
Climate Cool Continental
Key Grapes Pinot Grigio, Glera
Our Wine from Here
Monte degli Angeli Pinot Grigio 2024

Sicily

Southern Italy, Mediterranean Island
Italy's largest island is a treasure trove of indigenous grapes. Frappato produces light, aromatic reds perfect for chilling. Nerello Mascalese, usually for Etna reds, can be pressed immediately to make unique aromatic whites. Volcanic soils, sea breezes, and ancient varieties create wines unlike anywhere else.
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grapes Frappato, Nerello
Our Wines from Here
Gorghi Tondi Dume Frappato, Gorghi Tondi Rosa dei Venti

🇪🇸 Spain

Ribeira Sacra

Galicia, Northwest Spain
"Sacred Riverbank" is home to ancient monasteries and some of Europe's steepest vineyards. Mencía grapes cling to terraces along the Sil River canyon, requiring heroic viticulture. The cool, Atlantic-influenced climate produces elegant reds reminiscent of Pinot Noir - bright fruit, fresh acidity, and fine tannins.
Climate Atlantic
Key Grape Mencía
Our Wine from Here
Massimo Ribeira Sacra 2023

Rías Baixas

Galicia, Northwest Spain
Spain's premier white wine region sits on the Atlantic coast, where fjord-like inlets (rías) create a unique maritime climate. Albariño thrives on granite soils, producing aromatic whites with stone fruit, citrus, and distinctive salinity. The lush, rainy climate is more akin to Portugal than typical sunny Spain.
Climate Atlantic Maritime
Key Grape Albariño
Our Wine from Here
Marola Albariño Rías Baixas 2023

Penedès

Catalonia, Northeast Spain
Famous for Cava sparkling wine, Penedès also produces excellent still wines. High-altitude vineyards (400-600m) in areas like Font-Rubí give fresh, aromatic Garnacha with good acidity. The Mediterranean climate is moderated by elevation and sea breezes, creating balanced, food-friendly wines.
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grapes Garnacha, Macabeo
Our Wine from Here
Garnatxa Meretat Mont-Rubí 2022

Montsant

Catalonia, Northeast Spain
Surrounding the famous Priorat region, Montsant shares its dramatic slate (llicorella) soils and rugged terrain. Often called "baby Priorat," the wines offer similar power and mineral intensity at friendlier prices. Old vine Grenache and Carignan produce full-bodied, complex reds with Mediterranean herb character.
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grapes Grenache, Carignan
Our Wine from Here
Les Cousins L'Inconscient 2023

Castilla-La Mancha

Central Spain
Spain's wine heartland - the vast plains south of Madrid produce more wine than any other region in the world. The continental climate (hot summers, cold winters) and limestone soils create ripe, approachable Tempranillo. Excellent value wines that showcase Spain's noble grape in an easy-drinking style.
Climate Continental
Key Grape Tempranillo
Our Wine from Here
Encomienda de Cervera Horten 2023

Calatayud

Aragón, Northeast Spain
High in the mountains of Aragón (800-1000m), Calatayud produces some of Spain's most concentrated Garnacha. Old vines on rocky, slate-rich soils yield powerful wines with remarkable freshness from the altitude. The extreme continental climate creates intensity and character in these age-worthy reds.
Climate High-altitude Continental
Key Grape Garnacha
Our Wine from Here
Palmeri Navalta Farnacha 2018

🇩🇪 Germany

Rheinhessen

Western Germany
Germany's largest wine region is experiencing a quality renaissance. The Selztal (Selz Valley) produces aromatic wines from lesser-known varieties like Scheurebe. This Riesling crossing (created in 1916) offers exotic tropical and grapefruit notes. Warmer than the Mosel, Rheinhessen ripens aromatic varieties beautifully.
Climate Moderate Continental
Key Grapes Riesling, Scheurebe
Our Wine from Here
Braunewell Unser Täglich Scheurebe 2023

🇸🇮 Slovenia

Goriška Brda

Western Slovenia, Italian Border
Slovenia's most prestigious wine region shares the same hills as Italy's Collio - the border runs right through the vineyards. Cool climate and flysch soils produce elegant, European-style wines. Pinot Noir thrives here, showing Old World restraint with bright cherry fruit and silky tannins.
Climate Cool Continental
Key Grapes Pinot Noir, Rebula
Our Wine from Here
Krasno Pinot Noir 2022

🇺🇸 United States

Santa Barbara County

California Central Coast
One of California's coolest wine regions, Santa Barbara's transverse mountain ranges funnel Pacific fog inland. The Santa Maria Valley produces elegant Chardonnay with both richness and freshness - a balance between opulent Napa and lean Burgundy styles. Cool nights preserve acidity even as warm days ripen fruit.
Climate Cool Maritime
Key Grapes Chardonnay, Pinot Noir
Our Wine from Here
Presqu'ile Chardonnay 2023

🇨🇱 Chile

Central Valley

Central Chile
Chile's wine heartland runs between the Andes and the Pacific. The country's isolation (desert north, mountains east, ocean west, ice south) kept it phylloxera-free. Warm days and cool Pacific nights create wines with ripe fruit and fresh acidity. Excellent value Cabernet Sauvignon thrives in the Mediterranean climate.
Climate Mediterranean
Key Grapes Cab Sauv, Carmenère
Our Wine from Here
Orsa Cabernet Sauvignon 2020
